Case 001 · 2025

From a brief about heat pumps, a brand about hope.

Helio came to us as a five-letter folder on a partner's laptop — three businesses, two acquisitions, and one quietly ambitious thesis about decarbonising the world's least glamorous machinery. We named the company, drew the wordmark, wrote the words, and built the website it walks into the room with.

The brief

"We've quietly bought three of the most unloved companies in heating. Help us put them under one roof, and one name. We don't want to sound like a tech start-up. We don't want to sound like a utility, either."

— A. Mendes, founding partner, Helio Holdings

The first idea was a portmanteau. We binned it.

For three weeks the working name was a perfectly serviceable mash-up of two Latin roots, the kind of thing that survives a board deck and dies in a dinner conversation. Around week four, someone said the word sun out loud, in the wrong language, and the room went quiet. Helio is what you call a holding company you'd actually like to mention at parties.

The identity follows the name's economy. One wordmark, one round form, one structural rule: the shape of the sun gets drawn in the colour of whatever industry it's eating next. Black for transmission. Coral for residential heat. Lime for industrial cooling. The rest of the system is mostly type — pages that look like the company's investor letter, because that's what its readers grew up reading.

"The wordmark is the most expensive thing in the room. Everything else has to earn its place around it."
— Internal note, week 6

How it was made

Fourteen weeks, four phases, one held breath.

Most engagements at the studio look like this. The first phase is the one nobody schedules for, and the one we always charge for.

Phase 01

Listen. Re-write the brief.

Three weeks of interviews — operators, plumbers, the CFO, two unhappy former customers — and one quiet Friday rewriting the brief into a single paragraph the founder could sign.

Weeks 1 — 3
Phase 02

Find the name.

Six routes, sixty candidates, four legal clearances, one painful conversation with the partner who'd already started saying the old name. Helio arrived on day 24.

Weeks 3 — 6
Phase 03

Draw the mark.

Three rounds, twelve weights of "o," one borrowed letterform, and a wordmark that survives being engraved on a heat-pump fascia. Final on R3.

Weeks 6 — 10
Phase 04

Ship the system.

Brand book, voice manual, a one-page set of rules, and the public-facing site in twelve careful pages. Soft launch the day of the closing.

Weeks 10 — 14
